\chapter{Index of Notations}
Of course there is the usual collection of variable notations: $A, B$ for abelian categories or rings, functors or sheaves, $X,Y$ for preschemes, $x,y$ for points of them, $f,g$ for morphisms of preschemes, $\phi$ for morphisms of functors on sheaves, etc.


In general no underline denotes a group, as $\Hom(\sF, \sG)$, one underline denotes a sheaf, as $\underline{\Hom}(\sF, \sG)$ or $\underline{\Tor}(\sF, \sG)$, two underlines denotes an object in a derived category, as $\DerR\Hom^\bullet(\sF^\bullet, \sG^\bullet)$, and a dot denotes a complex.

In the list of stable notations below, we have distinguished five categories: Latin alphabet, other alphabets, superscripts, subscripts, and arbitrary symbols.
